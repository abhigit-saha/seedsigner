# BIP-39 Protocol Overview for SeedSigner Users

## What is BIP-39?

[BIP-39](https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0039.mediawiki) (Bitcoin Improvement Proposal 39) is the standard that defines how your SeedSigner generates those 12 or 24 word seed phrases. It converts random data into human-readable words that can backup your entire Bitcoin wallet.

## How BIP-39 Works

When you create a new seed on your SeedSigner:

`Generate 128-256 bits of entropy` → `Add checksum bits` → `Convert to 11-bit groups` → `Map to BIP-39 wordlist`

Your SeedSigner uses dice rolls or other entropy sources to create random data. This gets converted into seed words using the official [2048-word BIP-39 dictionary](https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0039/english.txt).

The seed phrase undergoes PBKDF2 processing to create a master seed that generates all your Bitcoin addresses following the [BIP-32 hierarchical deterministic standard](https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0032.mediawiki).

## Seed Length Options

- **12 words**: 128 bits entropy (standard security)
- **24 words**: 256 bits entropy (maximum security)

## Seedsigner-specific Information
The library used by Seedsigner to handle seed-phrases is [`embit`](https://github.com/diybitcoinhardware/embit).
Currently the only supported wordlist is English, with plans to support wordlists in other languages.
When we generate entropy and encode that entropy to a Seed, we map it to Seed-phrases. By default it maps to the default English one, however we can also have it map to seed words of other languages.
**Crucially, it means that the same entropy doesn't necessarily mean the same seed-phrase, according to the BIP-39 spec.**

So if you want two seed-phrases using the same inherent entropy (either using the same combination of dice-rolls or some other method), i.e. creating a seed using two different languages, the seed-phrase in those two different languages would be **non-complementary** i.e. you cannot back-up the seed-phrase of English using the seed-phrase in Spanish (for example) and vice versa.

Due to the non-complementary nature of non-English BIP-39 wordlists, any implementation of it in Seedsigner in the future would be kept inside the Advanced Options.

## Passphrase
Passphrase is an extra word introduced in BIP-39 that allows you to create multiple correct wallets from the same seed-phrase. This extra word is optional, however it is recommended as it introduces additional randomness, as well as introduce **Plausable Deniability**.

Adding a passphrase to a valid seed phrase loads up a completely correct and distinct Seed for each separate passphrase. This prevents the seed-phrase from being a single point of failure. Any bad actor would still have to correctly guess the passphrase to have access to a wallet, and since each passphrase generates a correct wallet, every single solution is plausible, although most incorrect, thus making the process of brute force much harder.

Passphrase should be treated as a very important password. Obvious precautions to keep are:
- Shouldn't be related in any way to your life
- Shouldn't be available in public literature (i.e. No lyrics of songs, No lines from books)
- You should be able to memorize it. (Contrary to passwords, you can afford to keep the passphrase short, i.e. around 8 letters. This is if you keep your seed-phrase very secure.)

If you feel you cannot remember your passphrase, or you want to have a longer passphrase, **you can write it down physically in a place separate from where you have written your seed phrase.**

## Pros of BIP-39

**Human-friendly backup**: Words are easier to write, read, and verify than random hex strings.

**Universal compatibility**: Your SeedSigner seed works with Electrum, Sparrow, Coldcard, Ledger, Trezor, and virtually every other Bitcoin wallet.

**Built-in error detection**: The checksum catches typos when you enter your seed phrase.

**Offline generation**: Your SeedSigner can create seeds completely airgapped, with no network connection required.

**Deterministic recovery**: The same seed phrase always recreates the exact same wallet, with identical addresses and transaction history.

## Cons and Limitations

**Single point of failure**: If someone gets your 12/24 words, they control your Bitcoin. There's no "forgot password" option. This is why it is important to have a passphrase.

**Non-complementary wordlists**: Non-English wordlists produce seeds that are incompatible with seeds generated by English wordlists and by other non-English wordlists.

**Backup burden**: You're responsible for protecting those words. No company or government can help you recover them.

**Physical storage challenges**: Paper can burn, flood damage, or fade over time. You need durable backup solutions.

**User error risks**: Write down words in wrong order, misspell them, or lose the backup entirely = permanent loss of funds.

## Critical Precautions for SeedSigner Users

**Never connect to internet**: Keep your SeedSigner permanently offline. Network connections compromise the security model.

**Use quality entropy**: Don't rush the dice rolling or coin-flipping process. Use proper casino dice / fair coin.

**Test on testnet first**: Generate practice wallets, send test transactions, practice recovery. Never learn on mainnet with real Bitcoin.

**Secure your backups**: Use metal storage, multiple geographic locations, and limit access. Test recovery annually.

**Your seed phrase = your Bitcoin**: Protect it like cash or gold.
